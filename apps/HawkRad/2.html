Simulation 2

Empty Space is Not Empty

Quantum Uncertainty states that in small perspectives of space, energy levels cannot be definitely certain. As a result, empty space is filled with a quantum soap of energy fluctuations, created from energy borrowed from nothing. These fluctuations often create pairs of virtual particles which collide and annihilate each other, giving their temporary energy back to nothingness.

This simulations now includes uncertainty and virtual particle pairs. Use the slider to increase uncertainty and cause virtual particles to randomly spawn. The pairs will converge and disappear upon meeting.
