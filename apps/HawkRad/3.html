Simulation 3

Virtual Particles at Event Horizons

At last, we put the two beasts together, the black holes of general relativity and fluctuations of quantum theory. Fluctuations happen everywhere, in and out of black holes. Most of the time, these fluctuations stay on either side, but now and again, a particle in a pair will get caught in the event horizon while the other remains free. This freed particle becomes a real photon and radiates away. This is Hawking radiation.

Create a virtual pair on the edge of the event horizon to watch this effect. Now increase uncertainty and watch this effect occurs automatically, as in nature.
